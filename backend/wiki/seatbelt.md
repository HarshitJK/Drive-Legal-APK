# Seatbelt — Section 194B, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Wearing a seatbelt is mandatory for all occupants of a motor vehicle under **Section 194B** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (as amended in 2019).

## Who Must Wear a Seatbelt?
- The **driver** of every motor vehicle (mandatory).
- All **front-seat passengers**.
- All **rear-seat passengers** (mandatory after 2019 amendment).
- Children must use child safety seats (under 4 years of age).

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| Driver not wearing seatbelt | ₹1,000 |
| Passenger not wearing seatbelt | ₹1,000 per passenger |
| Child not in child seat | ₹1,000 + licence deducted points |

## Additional Consequences
- Repeated non-compliance can lead to licence suspension.
- In case of an accident without seatbelt, insurance claim can be reduced.

## Types of Seatbelts Required
- **2-point belts**: No longer acceptable in most cars.
- **3-point (lap + shoulder) belts**: Standard requirement.
- All seats must have 3-point belts in new vehicles (from 2019 norms).

## State Variations
- **Delhi**: Very strict enforcement. Challans issued by cameras and e-surveillance.
- **Maharashtra**: Regular enforcement especially on highways.
- **Tamil Nadu**: Active seatbelt drives on NH and expressways.

## Child Safety
- Children under 4 years must be in a rear-facing child seat.
- Children 4–12 years must use a forward-facing child seat with harness.
- Carrying a child in the front seat is prohibited under age 12.

## Key Points
- "I was sitting in the back" is not a valid defence — rear seatbelts are mandatory.
- Airbags are not a replacement for seatbelts.
- Seatbelts reduce fatality risk by 45–60% in accidents.
