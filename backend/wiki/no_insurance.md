# No Insurance (Driving Without Insurance) — Section 196, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Every motor vehicle on a public road **must** have valid third-party insurance under **Section 146** of the MV Act. Driving without insurance is punishable under **Section 196**.

## Mandatory Insurance
- **Third-party liability insurance** is the minimum legal requirement.
- Covers damage or injury caused to other people and property.
- Comprehensive insurance is optional but recommended.

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| First offense | ₹2,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹4,000 |

## Additional Consequences
- Imprisonment up to **3 months** possible.
- Vehicle can be **seized/impounded** on the spot.
- Licence suspension possible.
- If you cause an accident without insurance, you are personally liable for **all damages**.
- No claim from Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT) if uninsured.

## How to Check Insurance Validity
- SMS: Send vehicle registration number to 7738299899 (VAHAN service).
- Online: Visit vahan.parivahan.gov.in and check via registration number.
- Policy copy is required while driving.

## Key Points
- Even if your vehicle is old or not used daily, it must be insured.
- Insurance that expired even one day ago counts as "no insurance".
- Online renewal takes 5 minutes — don't delay.
- Third-party insurance costs ₹700–₹1,500 per year for two-wheelers.
