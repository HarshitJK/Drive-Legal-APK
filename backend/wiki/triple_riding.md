# Triple Riding (Overloading Two-Wheelers) — Section 128, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Carrying more than one pillion passenger on a two-wheeler (triple riding) is an offence under **Section 128** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

## Rule
- A two-wheeler is designed to carry a maximum of **two persons** (driver + one pillion).
- Carrying a third person is illegal regardless of age or weight.

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| First offense | ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹2,000 – ₹4,000 |

## Additional Consequences
- Driving licence can be suspended.
- Vehicle may be seized temporarily.
- Insurance coverage can be voided in case of an accident with three riders.

## Child on Two-Wheelers
- A child accompanying a parent counts as a passenger.
- Children under 4 years of age: allowed in front of the rider in special seats (some states).
- Children 4 years and above: count as a full passenger; helmet required.

## Key Points
- Even short distances — "just around the corner" — can attract a challan.
- Traffic cameras now detect triple riding automatically.
- Overloaded two-wheelers are a major cause of road accidents.
