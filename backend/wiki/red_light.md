# Red Light Jumping — Section 119 / Section 177, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Jumping a red traffic light is an offence under **Section 119** read with **Section 177** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. It is one of the most common and dangerous traffic violations.

## What Counts as Red Light Jumping?
- Crossing the stop line when the signal is red.
- Entering the intersection before the signal turns green.
- Turning left when there is a red light and no "Left turn on red" sign.
- Crossing the stop line even if you stop inside the box.

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| First offense | ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹2,000 – ₹10,000 |

## Additional Consequences
- Driving licence can be suspended for repeat offenders.
- Court summons in some states.
- Traffic points deducted from licence.

## Automated Enforcement
- **ATCC cameras** (Automated Traffic Challan Cameras) detect red light violations automatically.
- Challan is generated and sent to the registered vehicle owner's address.
- No police officer needed — camera footage is evidence.

## State Variations
- **Delhi**: ATCC cameras at 100+ intersections. Fines delivered by post.
- **Mumbai**: Cameras at all major junctions; fines ₹1,000 – ₹5,000.
- **Bangalore**: Smart city cameras deployed at most major signals.
- **Chennai**: Traffic police issue on-spot challans and camera challans.

## Key Points
- Even if no police officer is present, cameras are watching.
- "Amber (yellow) light" means prepare to stop — not an invitation to speed up.
- Emergency vehicles (ambulance, fire brigade) may cross red lights legally.
