# Juvenile Driving — Section 199A, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Allowing a person under 18 years of age to drive a motor vehicle is a serious offence under **Section 199A** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (added by the 2019 Amendment).

## The Offence
- A minor (under 18) is not eligible for a driving licence.
- Driving a motor vehicle without a licence by a minor attracts penalties under both the minor AND the registered owner.

## Penalties on the Vehicle Owner / Parent / Guardian

| Offense | Fine | Other Penalty |
|---------|------|---------------|
| Minor driving with owner's knowledge | ₹25,000 | Owner treated as accused |
| Vehicle registration cancelled | — | RC cancelled |
| Owner's licence cancelled | — | DL cancelled |
| Minor tried under JJ Act | — | Court process |

## Juvenile's Penalty
- The minor is tried as a **juvenile delinquent** under the Juvenile Justice Act.
- The motor vehicle is not allowed to be registered under the minor's name until they turn 18.

## Key Points
- Parents who give car/bike keys to children under 18 face ₹25,000 fine AND their own DL and vehicle RC can be cancelled.
- "My child was just driving in the colony" is not a defence — any public road counts.
- The vehicle owner (parent) is held accountable even if they were not present.
- Minimum age for a learner's licence is 18 years (16 for mopeds below 50cc).

## State Variations
- All states follow the central MV Act for juvenile driving.
- Some states like **Delhi** and **Maharashtra** run specific drives targeting school/college areas.

## Payment / Court
Juvenile driving cases require court appearance. Challan details at echallan.parivahan.gov.in
