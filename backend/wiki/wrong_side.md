# Wrong Side Driving — Section 184, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Driving on the wrong side of the road is a **dangerous driving** offence under **Section 184** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

## What is Wrong Side Driving?
- Driving against the designated direction of traffic flow.
- Overtaking using the oncoming traffic lane and not returning.
- Taking U-turns from the wrong lane.
- Driving on one-way roads in the prohibited direction.

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| First offense | ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹2,000 – ₹10,000 |

## Additional Consequences
- Driving licence suspension for repeat offenders.
- Court summons for causing accidents while driving on the wrong side.
- Vehicle detention.

## Common Wrong-Side Scenarios
1. Entering a highway from the wrong ramp direction.
2. Driving against traffic on a one-way street.
3. Using the emergency lane as a driving lane in the opposite direction.
4. Cutting across median strips (dividers).

## State Variations
- **Delhi**: Heavy fines on Ring Road and highways; cameras and police deployed.
- **Mumbai**: Active enforcement on expressways and flyovers.
- **Hyderabad**: Dedicated lane discipline drives on ORR (Outer Ring Road).

## Key Points
- Wrong side driving kills more people per accident than almost any other violation.
- Even if you are in a hurry, there is no justification for wrong-side driving.
- GPS navigation systems do not excuse wrong-side entry on one-way roads.
