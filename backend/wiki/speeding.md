# Speeding — Section 183, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Driving at a speed exceeding the posted speed limit is an offence under **Section 183** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (amended 2019).

## Speed Limits (General)

| Road Type | Light Motor Vehicle (LMV) | Heavy Motor Vehicle (HMV) | Two-Wheeler |
|-----------|--------------------------|--------------------------|-------------|
| National Highway | 100 km/h | 80 km/h | 80 km/h |
| State Highway | 90 km/h | 70 km/h | 70 km/h |
| Urban Roads | 50 km/h | 40 km/h | 50 km/h |
| Near Schools/Hospitals | 25 km/h | 25 km/h | 25 km/h |
| Expressways | 120 km/h | 100 km/h | 80 km/h |

## Fines (as per MV Amendment Act 2019)

| Offense | LMV Fine | HMV Fine |
|---------|----------|----------|
| First offense | ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 | ₹2,000 – ₹4,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹2,000 – ₹4,000 | ₹4,000 – ₹8,000 |

- Two-wheelers and three-wheelers follow LMV rates.
- Racing/excessive speeding (Section 189): ₹5,000 – ₹10,000 and licence suspension.

## Additional Penalties
- Licence suspension for serious speeding violations.
- Court summons for repeat offences.
- Vehicle impoundment in extreme cases.

## Key Points
- Speed limits are enforced by speed cameras (ATCC) on national highways.
- Challans are issued electronically via Vahan/Parivahan systems.
- You can check and pay your challan at echallan.parivahan.gov.in
