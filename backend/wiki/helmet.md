# Helmet — Section 129, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Wearing a protective helmet is mandatory for **all riders and pillion passengers** on two-wheelers under **Section 129** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

## Who Must Wear a Helmet?
- The driver (rider) of every motorised two-wheeler.
- Every pillion passenger on a two-wheeler.
- Children above 4 years of age riding as pillion.

## Helmet Standards
- Helmets must conform to **BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards)** specifications.
- Only ISI-marked helmets are legally acceptable.
- Half-face helmets are allowed but full-face helmets are recommended.

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| First offense | ₹1,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹1,000 (plus possible DL disqualification for 3 months) |

## Additional Consequences
- Driving licence can be disqualified for **3 months** on repeat offences.
- Traffic police can seize the vehicle temporarily.
- Challans can be issued by e-surveillance cameras.

## Exemptions
- Sikh persons wearing a turban are exempted from the helmet requirement under Section 129.
- Persons with medical conditions (with doctor's certificate) may apply for exemption.

## State Variations
- **Tamil Nadu**: ₹1,000 fine; no DL suspension for first offence.
- **Delhi**: Strict enforcement; DL suspension possible for repeat offenders.
- **Maharashtra**: Heavy enforcement; fines collected on-spot.

## Key Points
- Do not buy non-ISI helmets to save money — they offer no real protection and can attract a fine.
- Challans are issued instantly via body cameras worn by traffic police.
- Pay challans at echallan.parivahan.gov.in
