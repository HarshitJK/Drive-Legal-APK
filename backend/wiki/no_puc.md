# PUC (Pollution Under Control) Certificate — Section 190(2), Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Every motor vehicle must carry a valid **Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate** as per **Section 190(2)** of the MV Act. This ensures the vehicle meets emission standards.

## Who Must Have PUC?
- All petrol, diesel, and CNG vehicles.
- Both private and commercial vehicles.
- Two-wheelers, three-wheelers, four-wheelers, and heavy vehicles.

## Validity
| Vehicle Age | Validity |
|-------------|----------|
| New vehicle (first 1 year) | 1 year (covered by manufacturer) |
| After 1 year | Renewed every 6 months |
| BS6 vehicles | 1 year validity per renewal |

## Where to Get PUC
- Any authorised PUC testing centre (typically at petrol pumps).
- Cost: ₹60 – ₹100 per test.
- Takes about 5 minutes; certificate issued immediately.

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| Driving without valid PUC | ₹10,000 |
| Repeat offense | ₹10,000 |

## Additional Consequences
- Vehicle can be seized until PUC is produced.
- Vehicle registration can be suspended.
- In Delhi NCR, vehicles without PUC can be challaned and impounded.

## Checking PUC Status
- Online: vahan.parivahan.gov.in
- DigiLocker stores PUC certificate digitally.

## State Variations
- **Delhi**: Very strict PUC enforcement due to air quality concerns. Vehicles seized on-spot.
- **Maharashtra**: Regular PUC checks at toll nakas.
- **Karnataka**: Automated PUC cameras at some intersections.

## Key Points
- An expired PUC even by one day leads to ₹10,000 fine.
- PUC must be carried physically or in DigiLocker.
- Electric vehicles (EVs) are exempt from PUC requirements.
