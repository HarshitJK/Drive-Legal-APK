# No Driving Licence — Section 3 / Section 181, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Every person who drives a motor vehicle on a public road **must hold a valid driving licence** under **Section 3** of the MV Act. Driving without a licence is punishable under **Section 181**.

## Types of Driving Licences
- **Learner's Licence (LL)**: Valid for 6 months. Must display "L" plate. Must be accompanied by holder of permanent licence.
- **Permanent Driving Licence (DL)**: Issued after passing the driving test.
- **Commercial Licence**: Required for transport vehicles (Section 7).

## Fines

| Offense | Fine |
|---------|------|
| Driving without any licence | ₹5,000 |
| Driving with an expired licence | ₹500 – ₹1,500 |
| Driving without L-plate (learner) | ₹500 |
| Allowing minor/unlicensed to drive | ₹5,000 + owner's DL cancelled |

## Additional Consequences
- Imprisonment up to **3 months** for driving without a licence.
- Vehicle impoundment until valid licence is produced.
- RC (Registration Certificate) of the vehicle owner can be cancelled.
- Insurance claims can be rejected if driver had no licence at time of accident.

## How to Check Licence Validity
- Online: sarathi.parivahan.gov.in
- DigiLocker app has digital DL accepted by traffic police.

## Licence Renewal
- DL must be renewed before expiry (typically valid for 20 years until age 50, then 5 years).
- Grace period of 30 days after expiry; after that it is treated as "no licence".

## Key Points
- A DigiLocker digital copy of your DL is legally valid.
- An expired DL is treated differently from "no licence" — fine is lower.
- If you lend your vehicle to someone without a licence, both you and they are penalised.
