# Tamil Nadu — State Traffic Laws & Fine Schedule

## Overview
Tamil Nadu follows the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act 2019 as the baseline. The state has enacted some specific modifications and has an active e-challan system.

## Tamil Nadu E-Challan System
- Online portal: **tnemergency.tn.gov.in** and **echallan.parivahan.gov.in**
- Challans issued through ATCC cameras and police mobile units.
- Payment via net banking, UPI, or at Arasu Parivahan offices.

## Key Fine Schedule (Tamil Nadu Specific)

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Speeding | ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 (first); ₹2,000 – ₹4,000 (repeat) |
| Helmet | ₹1,000 (first); licence disqualification possible |
| Drunk Driving | ₹10,000 (first); ₹15,000 (repeat) |
| No Insurance | ₹2,000 (first); ₹4,000 (repeat) |
| No Licence | ₹5,000 |
| No PUC | ₹10,000 |
| Seatbelt | ₹1,000 |
| Mobile Phone | ₹5,000 |
| Red Light | ₹1,000 – ₹5,000 |
| Triple Riding | ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 |

## Special Rules in Tamil Nadu
- **Helmet enforcement**: Strict in Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Tiruchirappalli.
- **School zones**: 25 km/h mandatory near schools; additional ₹500 fine.
- **Expressways**: The Chennai Outer Ring Road (ORR) has speed limits of 80 km/h.
- **Bus lane violations**: ₹500 – ₹1,000 in Chennai bus rapid transit zones.

## Challan Payment
- Pay at any District Collectorate or online at echallan.parivahan.gov.in
- Challans must be paid within 60 days or a court summons is issued.
