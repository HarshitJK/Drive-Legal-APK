# Drunk Driving (DUI) — Section 185, Motor Vehicles Act 1988

## Overview
Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs is one of the most serious traffic offences in India, covered under **Section 185** of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

## Legal Limit
- Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC) limit: **30 mg per 100 ml of blood**
- Breath test limit: **150 micrograms of alcohol per litre of breath**

## Fines and Penalties

| Offense | Fine | Imprisonment |
|---------|------|--------------|
| First offense | ₹10,000 | Up to 6 months |
| Repeat offense (within 3 years) | ₹15,000 | Up to 2 years |

## Additional Consequences
- **Driving licence suspended** for 6 months on first offence.
- **Permanent cancellation** of licence is possible for repeat offenders.
- **Vehicle impoundment** for 7 days minimum.
- Criminal record under IPC for causing accidents while drunk.
- **Court case** mandatory — cannot pay and walk away.

## Drug-Impaired Driving
- Section 185 also covers driving under the influence of drugs.
- Police can require a blood/urine test.
- Refusal to take the test is treated as an admission of guilt.

## State Variations
- **Delhi**: Strict enforcement with roadside Breath Analyser checks. DL suspended immediately.
- **Maharashtra**: ₹10,000 fine + possible 6-month imprisonment on first offence. Vehicles seized.
- **Punjab**: Special anti-drunk driving drives on weekends.
- **Karnataka**: Nighttime checkpoints enforced regularly.

## Key Points
- You CANNOT pay the challan and avoid court appearance for drunk driving — it is a cognizable offence.
- Even 1–2 drinks may exceed the 30 mg/100 ml BAC limit.
- Refusing a breath analyser test can lead to arrest.
- Zero tolerance for commercial vehicle drivers under Section 185A.

## Payment
Drunk driving challans require court appearance. Challan details at echallan.parivahan.gov.in
